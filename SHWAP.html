SHWAP

Water Wastage Prevention System. Alerts owners when they forget to turn off their hoses after watering. Encourages responsible water management, fosters water conservation, and contributes to a sustainable environment.

Role: lead engineer, programmer, and team manager

Skills: Prototyping, Design, Coding, Project Management, Arduino

Awards: State championship and sponsored by Deloitte @ NM Governor's STEM Challenge

Problem
Purpose
Objective
I recognized that forgetfulness contributes to significant water wastage, particularly when it comes to garden hoses left running after watering in my community (3/5 team members experienced this issue). The effects are particularly devastating in desert regions, especially with water scarcity. Improper management of water resources increases environmental challenges and costly water bills.
Address the problem of water wastage caused by forgetfulness in turning off garden hoses by developing a solution that alerts users when their hoses are left running. The project aims to promote responsible water management, encourage water conservation, and contribute to a sustainable environment.
Devlop an alarm notification device. It must be cost-effective, and the entire development process should adhere to a budget of $500 while maintaining a minimum 90% success rate in accurately alerting users about water wastage.

Design Process

After surveying 100 participants, we found that 88% had experienced or knew someone who had incidents of forgetting to turn off their hoses, resulting in substantial water wastage. Understanding the severity of this issue, we started developing the prototype.


In choosing components for SHWAP, our primary goals were simplicity, accommodation for various types of users, and accuracy. To cater to individuals with visual or auditory impairments, we included two notification methods: visual and auditory. We intentionally kept the mechanism compact for portability and used a durable, weather resistance electrical box to protect the components from water damage and harsh weather.

For seamless operation, a water flow sensor was selected, allowing one end to connect to the hose nozzle and the other end to attach to the hose with a connector. Users can customize the duration before notifications are triggered, accommodating different watering needs based on garden size. For instance, if water flow is detected for a specified amount of time, such as five minutes or more, notifications will be activated. Our final design incorporated an Arduino, LED, piezo buzzer, water flow sensor, and battery to create a functional and user-friendly device.

Prototype

Demo

For demonstration purposes, the time constraint was set to 30 seconds. When water flow was detected for 30 seconds or longer, the LED and buzzer were activated, effectively notifying users of the prolonged water usage.

Test Results

We conducted real-world testing, incorporating the mechanism with a hose, and completed a total of 15 trials to assess its practicality and reliability. The initial prototype demonstrated a success rate of 92% with a corresponding 8% failure rate, reflecting strong performance.

Future Devlopments

Waterproof the exterior (currently water-resistant): including the cable and LED that pass through an opening.

Insulate the interior of the container, which houses the circuitry.

Implement solar power to improve battery duration.

Create a software system that manages notifications and monitors the device.
